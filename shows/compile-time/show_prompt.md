## Objective

Complete the detailed design of the three-movement competitive marching arts show "Compile Time," translating locked concept decisions into precise drill formations, synchronized music composition, and integrated guard choreography. All elements must reinforce circuit/data-flow imagery and the error-to-success narrative arc across exact yard-line coordinates, musical bars, and timing cues.

## Deliverables

- **Drill Design (Yard-Line Precision)**
  - **Movement 1:** Complete 8×1 → 8×6 linear grid expansion
    - Starting yard-line position
    - Expansion path (straight columns advancing outward) with entrance timing for each row
    - Final 8×6 formation coordinate locations
    - Music sync: grid adds one row per phrase entry (7 total phrase entries for 6 expansions)
  
  - **Movement 2:** Fragmentation and reassembly sequences
    - Identify 3–5 diagonal fragment pathways cutting through the grid
    - Reassembly formations (clean perpendicular patterns) after each fragment
    - Jagged angle specifications (recommend 30°/45°/60° diagonals)
    - Guard flag figure-8 positioning relative to fragments
    - Music sync: fragment entries and reassembly timing to dissonance/resolution moments
  
  - **Movement 3:** Concentric circles convergence
    - Define 3–4 concentric circle formations with specific radii
    - Center point location (recommended: 50-yard line, hash mark)
    - Convergence pacing from outermost ring to center
    - Guard integration pathway (weaving between circles)
    - Music sync: convergence timing to unison crystallization climax

- **Music Composition (Full Score)**
  - **Movement 1 (3 minutes, 120 BPM = 360 beats)**
    - Piccolo/clarinet unison foundation (bars 1–8, 16 beats)
    - Trombone fourths entry (bar 8, stacking underneath)
    - Additional woodwind/brass layers per phrase (specify instrumentation)
    - Brass tutti crystallization at 1:45 (bar 44–45), key shift up a fourth
    - Notation: specify all voicings (fourths/fifths stacking structure)
  
  - **Movement 2 (4 minutes, 120 BPM = 480 beats)**
    - Opening dissonance block (cluster chords, specify pitch/rhythm)
    - Fragmented phrasing sections with rhythmic disruption and selective silence
    - Reassembly moments (harmonic resolution or partial clarity)
    - Guard figure-8 sync cues embedded in score (tie to percussion hits or phrase endings)
    - Maintain tension until final reassembly
  
  - **Movement 3 (3 minutes, 120 BPM = 360 beats)**
    - Opening from key-shifted Movement 2 exit
    - Full ensemble unison/octave doublings building to climax
    - Harmonic resolution resolving all dissonance from Movement 2
    - Alignment with concentric convergence pacing (climax at center point reach)

- **Guard Choreography & Specifications**
  - **Movement 1:** Neutral flag positioning (not featured; integration builds in later movements)
  
  - **Movement 2 Figure-8 Execution:**
    - Horizontal figure-8 pattern specification (amplitude, rotation speed, timing)
    - Entry timing relative to dissonance block
    - Red silk catch moments (tie to specific jagged drill angles or reassembly moments)
    - Exit timing before green transition
    - Position relative to fragmented drill formations (asymmetric placement)
  
  - **Movement 3 Integration:**
    - Guard entry into concentric circles as circuit data
    - Specific weaving pathways between rings (number of spirals, direction, speed)
    - Green flag catch timing (sync to unison crystallization)
    - Final position at convergence completion (on center point or circling center?)
    - Flag heights and arcs during movement through formations
  
  - **Color & Material Specifications:**
    - Red silk: exact pantone/hex specification, finish preference (matte/glossy/iridescent)
    - Green silk: exact pantone/hex specification, finish preference
    - Flag dimensions (height × width in inches)
    - Weight/material composition recommendation (nylon blend, silk blend, etc.)

- **Integration Document (Timing Sync)**
  - Movement-by-movement bar-by-bar timeline showing:
    - Music composition cues (bar numbers, key moments, dissonance/resolution, crystallization)
    - Drill formation transitions (entry timing, row-by-row grid expansion, fragment breaks, reassembly, convergence)
    - Guard choreography cues (figure-8 start/end bars, color transition timing, integration entries)
  - Precision tolerance verification (all sync points within ±2 beats, ±1 count acceptable variance)
  - Rehearsal pacing notes (tempo variations, if any, for musical expression vs. drill precision)

- **Visual Design Documentation**
  - Formation diagrams (top-down view) for key moments in each movement
    - Movement 1: 8×1, 8×2, 8×3, 8×4, 8×5, 8×6 configurations
    - Movement 2: 2–3 fragment states + reassembly formations
    - Movement 3: 2–3 concentric ring states + final center convergence
  - Circuit board/data flow reference imagery (annotate how formations reflect these metaphors)
  - Guard positioning relative to drill in each key formation

## Constraints

- **Locked Parameters (do not modify):**
  - Ensemble size: 150 members total
  - Movement 1 grid: 8 marchers per line, expanding to 6 lines (48 brass total)
  - Movement durations: Mvt 1 = 3 min, Mvt 2 = 4 min, Mvt 3 = 3 min (hard timers)
  - Grid expansion path: Linear straight columns (not spiral or angular)
  - Formation transition tolerance: 4 counts (~2 seconds at 120 BPM)
  - Tempo baseline: 120 BPM
  - Guard integration: Full integration into Movement 3 (guard as circuit data)
  - Guard flag state: Red (Movement 1–2), green (Movement 3)
  - Guard choreography (Movement 2): Horizontal figure-8s mirroring jagged drill angles

- **Design Guidelines:**
  - All formations must be visually achievable within standard DCI spacing conventions
  - Guard weaving must not disrupt drill precision or create timing conflicts
  - Dissonance in Movement 2 must feel "controlled chaos," not random (tie to specific error metaphors—dropped phrases, rhythmic stutters, harmonic clusters)
  - Concentric circles must converge smoothly without abrupt changes of direction
  - Circuit imagery must be apparent in formation diagrams (label connections/data flows if helpful)
