# Winter Camps 2025 - Acceptance Criteria Framework
## Addressing Critique Feedback: Clear Functional Benchmarks

**Generated:** BASICS Mode Review Session
**Program Coordinator:** The Lindenhurst Legato Longtones

---

## PURPOSE

This document defines **clear, measurable acceptance criteria** for each major SET in the Winter Camps 2025 structure, directly addressing judge feedback:

> "Clarify 'functional' acceptance criteria in pre-show brief - Define the minimum viable end-to-end flow (message sending, state updates, visual feedback loop)"

---

## MOVEMENT 1: FOUNDATION AND FUNDAMENTALS

### SET: Basic Technique Warmups
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] 90% of ensemble can complete 8-minute warmup routine without stopping
- [ ] Posture checklist: shoulders relaxed, spine aligned, feet shoulder-width (observable by staff)
- [ ] Long tones held for 8+ counts with consistent tone quality
- [ ] Breathing exercises demonstrate visible diaphragm engagement

**Functional Integration:** Warmup routine becomes daily pre-rehearsal protocol for all subsequent movements.

---

### SET: Ensemble Alignment and Listening
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Ensemble can tune unison concert Bb within +/- 5 cents in under 3 minutes
- [ ] Sections can identify and correct blend issues in real-time during chord progression
- [ ] Call-and-response drills maintain rhythmic accuracy within 1/16 note
- [ ] Members can verbally identify when their section is too loud/soft relative to ensemble

**Functional Integration:** Listening skills apply directly to Movement 2 (Musical Development) repertoire work.

---

### SET: Brass Fundamental Skills
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Range exercises: all brass can play assigned part range with consistent tone
- [ ] Articulation patterns: clean tonguing at quarter note = 120 BPM minimum
- [ ] Endurance: sustained playing for 10+ minutes without significant tone degradation
- [ ] Intonation: section tuning accuracy within +/- 10 cents on held notes

**Functional Integration:** Skills directly enable Movement 2 repertoire performance standards.

---

### SET: Percussion Fundamental Skills
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Battery: All rudiments (singles, doubles, paradiddles) at 120 BPM with clean execution
- [ ] Front ensemble: 2-mallet scales at quarter note = 100 BPM, even tone across all bars
- [ ] Timing: Section plays together within 5ms tolerance (audibly unified)
- [ ] Dynamics: Demonstrated control from pp to ff with gradual crescendo/decrescendo

**Functional Integration:** Rudiments and timing precision required for Movement 2 musical passages.

---

### SET: Guard Equipment Fundamentals
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Flag work: Clean tosses with 80%+ catch rate during 3-minute sequence
- [ ] Rifle work: Single spins controlled, catches clean 75%+ of attempts
- [ ] Body positioning: Equipment path parallel to body, hands in correct positions
- [ ] Coordination: Equipment work synchronized with 8-count phrase structure

**Functional Integration:** Equipment proficiency required for Movement 3 visual program choreography.

---

## MOVEMENT 2: MUSICAL DEVELOPMENT

### SET: Repertoire Introduction
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] All members have received and reviewed full score/part assignments
- [ ] Note accuracy: 90%+ correct notes at slow tempo (quarter note = 60 BPM)
- [ ] Rhythm accuracy: Correct note values and rest placements
- [ ] Key signature awareness: No recurring accidental errors

**Functional Integration:** Foundation for Movement 4 full ensemble integration.

---

### SET: Musical Phrasing and Expression
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Phrases demonstrate clear breath points and musical direction
- [ ] Dynamic contrast: Minimum 3 dynamic levels observable (p, mf, f)
- [ ] Articulation variety: Staccato, legato, accents executed as marked
- [ ] Musical pacing: Tempo fluctuations intentional and controlled (+/- 5 BPM)

**Functional Integration:** Expressive playing enhances Movement 4 performance quality.

---

### SET: Section Integration and Blend
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Sections can perform musical passages together with unified sound
- [ ] Handoffs between sections are seamless (no gaps or overlaps)
- [ ] Balance: Melody audible, harmony supportive, bass foundation clear
- [ ] Intonation: Multi-section chords in tune within +/- 10 cents

**Functional Integration:** Multi-section work prepares for Movement 4 full ensemble rehearsals.

---

## MOVEMENT 3: VISUAL PROGRAM CONSTRUCTION

### SET: Drill Fundamentals and Grid Work
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Members can find grid coordinates within +/- 6 inches accuracy
- [ ] Step size: 8-to-5 steps within 1 inch accuracy over 16 counts
- [ ] Form awareness: Members know their position relative to nearest 4 performers
- [ ] Transition timing: Set changes completed within assigned count structure

**Functional Integration:** Drill accuracy enables Movement 4 music-visual synchronization.

---

### SET: Movement Vocabulary and Body Work
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Core movement vocabulary: 8+ moves performed with correct posture/technique
- [ ] Body angles: Head, torso, arms positioned as choreographed (90% consistency)
- [ ] Timing: Body movements hit musical accents within +/- 1 count
- [ ] Performance quality: Energy and facial expression appropriate to show theme

**Functional Integration:** Body technique combines with Movement 2 music for full performance.

---

### SET: Guard Equipment Technique
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Choreographed sequences: 80%+ catch rate during full routine
- [ ] Timing: Equipment releases/catches synchronized with musical phrases
- [ ] Visual effect: Equipment paths create intended visual shapes/pictures
- [ ] Safety: No equipment-related injuries or near-misses during full runs

**Functional Integration:** Guard visuals integrate with ensemble music in Movement 4.

---

### SET: Visual-Musical Integration Concepts
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Members can identify 3+ moments where visual enhances musical phrase
- [ ] Timing: Visual hits align with musical accents (within +/- 1 count)
- [ ] Energy matching: Visual intensity reflects musical dynamics
- [ ] Storytelling: Visual choices support overall show narrative

**Functional Integration:** Direct bridge to Movement 4 combined rehearsals.

---

## MOVEMENT 4: INTEGRATION AND PERFORMANCE PREPARATION

### SET: Full Ensemble Integration
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Complete run-through: Beginning to end without stopping
- [ ] Music-visual sync: Drill changes align with musical phrase structure
- [ ] Section balance: All sections audible in appropriate proportion
- [ ] Timing: Ensemble stays together within +/- 1 count throughout

**Functional Integration:** End-to-end performance demonstrates all prior work combined.

---

### SET: Performance Run-Throughs
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Stamina: Full show performed 2+ times consecutively without significant quality drop
- [ ] Consistency: 3+ consecutive runs with similar quality scores (staff assessment)
- [ ] Recovery: Ensemble can execute clean performance after break/reset
- [ ] Mental focus: Performers maintain concentration throughout full show

**Functional Integration:** Stamina and consistency required for competition-ready performance.

---

### SET: Feedback and Refinement Cycles
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Video review: 100% of ensemble has viewed and discussed performance footage
- [ ] Error identification: Members can articulate 2+ personal improvement areas
- [ ] Correction implementation: Identified errors reduced by 50%+ in next run
- [ ] Staff feedback: Written notes provided and acknowledged by all members

**Functional Integration:** Continuous improvement cycle ensures spring season readiness.

---

## END-TO-END FLOW: MINIMUM VIABLE PERFORMANCE BENCHMARK

**By end of Winter Camps 2025, ensemble must demonstrate:**

1. **Musical Proficiency:** All repertoire performed at 80%+ accuracy with appropriate dynamics and phrasing
2. **Visual Execution:** Drill executed with 85%+ form accuracy, transitions clean
3. **Integration:** Music and visual synchronized within +/- 2 counts throughout show
4. **Performance Quality:** Energy, expression, and storytelling elements present
5. **Stamina:** Full show performed 3x consecutively with consistent quality
6. **Self-Assessment:** Members can identify strengths/weaknesses and articulate improvement plan

**This benchmark represents "functional" readiness for Spring Season advancement.**

---

## VERIFICATION CHECKPOINTS

Each SET must pass functional verification before progression:

- **Movement 1 Checkpoint:** Can ensemble execute fundamental skills independently?
- **Movement 2 Checkpoint:** Can sections perform repertoire at performance tempo?
- **Movement 3 Checkpoint:** Can ensemble execute drill with music independently?
- **Movement 4 Checkpoint:** Is full show performance competition-ready?

**Program Coordinator will coordinate checkpoint assessments with caption heads.**
