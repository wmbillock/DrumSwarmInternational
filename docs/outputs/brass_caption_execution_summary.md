### 🟢 GREEN Phase — Implementation

**Long Tone and Lip Slur Protocol:**
1. Daily mouthpiece buzz: 5 minutes before instrument pickup
2. Long tone sequence: Concert F→Bb→Eb, 4 counts each at pp→ff
3. Lip slur progression: Harmonic partials 1-4 ascending and descending
4. Flexibility studies: Bb major scale slurred, quarter note = 60→96

**Articulation Implementation:**
1. Syllable mapping: tu (staccato), du (legato), dah (marcato), tah (tenuto)
2. Tongue placement: tongue tip on teeth ridge, consistent contact point
3. Air support: continuous air stream regardless of articulation style
4. Speed progression: 60→80→96→112→132 BPM with metronome

**Attack Consistency Protocol:**
1. Listen-before-play: 2 beats of mental preparation before attack
2. Conductor alignment: attacks timed to ictus, not anticipation
3. Section unison drills: tutti attacks with recording review
4. Recovery protocol: missed attacks corrected without stopping

**Tone Quality and Projection:**
1. Oral cavity: "open ah" vowel, lifted soft palate, fast air stream
2. Embouchure: firm corners, centered placement, minimal mouthpiece pressure
3. Air velocity: fast, supported air column from diaphragm
4. Bell angle: 10-degree upward tilt for press box projection

**Tone Centering:**
1. Reference pitch: Concert Bb from tuner, hold 4 counts
2. Resonance buzz on mouthpiece: centered pitch, full buzz
3. Tuner feedback: visual confirmation of center ±5 cents
4. Harmonic alignment: tune to 3rd and 5th partials of chord

**Vowel Shaping and Blend:**
1. Unified vowel catalog: "ah" (bright passages), "oh" (dark/warm), "ee" (upper register)
2. Mirror matching: section leaders demonstrate target vowel shape
3. Blend check: conductor listens from 30 yards, adjusts balance
4. Intonation alignment: tune after blend is established

**Musicality and Expression:**
1. Sing-then-play methodology: vocalize phrase shape before playing
2. Dynamic arc: mark crescendo/decrescendo in parts with pencil
3. Phrase breathing: coordinate breath points within sections
4. Interpretive intent: staff communicates emotional character of each phrase

**Tone Color and Style:**
1. March style: bright, projected, marcato articulation, "ah" vowel
2. Ballad style: warm, supported, legato, "oh" vowel, relaxed corners
3. Contemporary: flexible, aggressive air, varied syllables, wide dynamic range
4. Style drills: same passage performed in 3 different styles back-to-back
