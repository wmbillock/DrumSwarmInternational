## MOVEMENT 1: FOUNDATION AND FUNDAMENTALS
**Movement ID:** 7256eb9a-1630-4526-9955-460b40a33636

### Canonical Sets (Consolidated View):

#### SET 1: Basic Technique Warmups
**Primary Set ID:** b426ad93-415e-4c03-833b-9720e0cf738a

**Purpose:** Establish daily warmup routines covering breathing, long tones, scales, and basic marching technique

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- 100% of members can execute 8-minute warmup sequence independently
- Breath support maintains consistent tone for 16+ counts at mf-f dynamic
- Long tones show stable pitch (within 5 cents) and consistent timbre
- Percussion warmup pad routine demonstrates even stick heights and rhythmic accuracy
- All members demonstrate correct posture and instrument carriage

**Functional Benchmark:** Ensemble can begin any rehearsal with synchronized, efficient warmup without staff intervention

---

#### SET 2: Ensemble Alignment and Listening
**Primary Set ID:** a585dc3e-74a5-4add-b7f5-5ff3f50db3b1

**Purpose:** Develop tuning awareness, blend, balance, and ensemble timing skills

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Sections can tune to A440 reference within 30 seconds with <3 cent variance
- Members identify and adjust blend issues in real-time during sustained chords
- Balance exercises show dynamic awareness (melody vs. harmony voicing)
- Call-and-response drills demonstrate <0.5 beat timing accuracy
- Cutoffs are clean and synchronized within visual frame

**Functional Benchmark:** Ensemble demonstrates locked-in pitch center and responsive listening without constant staff correction

---

#### SET 3: Brass Fundamental Skills
**Primary Set ID:** 7e4d6b61-91ed-4581-9af1-1beb726e94ae

**Purpose:** Build brass tone quality, range development, articulation consistency, and endurance

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Consistent tone production across full written range (low to high register)
- Articulation patterns (legato, staccato, marcato) are distinct and clean
- Endurance allows 4+ minutes of continuous playing without significant tone degradation
- Dynamic control spans pp to ff with characteristic tone maintained
- Section unison passages sound as single voice (blend test)

**Functional Benchmark:** Brass sections perform all warmup material with professional tone quality and technical precision

---

#### SET 4: Percussion Fundamental Skills
**Primary Set ID:** 518b7a84-485a-4264-893f-84738e3f7ee7

**Purpose:** Establish percussion technique across battery and front ensemble

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Battery: All 40 PAS rudiments executed at 120+ BPM with proper stick heights
- Front Ensemble: 4-mallet technique shows independent control and accuracy
- Timing exercises lock to metronome within +/- 5ms variance
- Dynamic contrast ranges from pp to ff with control
- Grip consistency and stroke quality maintained across tempo ranges

**Functional Benchmark:** Percussion sections can execute fundamental exercises as teaching examples for new members

---

#### SET 5: Guard Equipment Fundamentals
**Primary Set ID:** b35d127b-b7af-428a-9dcf-228f5504a832

**Purpose:** Establish flag, rifle, and sabre basic technique and equipment safety

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Flag: Clean spins, tosses, and catches with consistent plane control
- Rifle: Safe handling protocols observed 100% of time, basic tosses land 80%+
- Sabre: Grip and basic figure-8 patterns show smooth, controlled motion
- Equipment matching: Section shows visual uniformity in timing and placement
- Drop rate during fundamental exercises: <10%

**Functional Benchmark:** Guard can perform basic equipment work safely and confidently during block staging

## MOVEMENT 2: MUSICAL DEVELOPMENT
**Movement ID:** 6db98a4b-473f-4e4e-8054-10e4297c749e

### Canonical Sets (Consolidated View):

#### SET 1: Repertoire Introduction by Section
**Primary Set ID:** a6e48a9f-fe32-4144-9213-c9e621144a62

**Purpose:** Initial learning of show music - note reading, rhythms, key signatures, basic structure

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- 100% of members have music and can identify their part
- Note accuracy: 95%+ correct pitches at slow tempo (60% of performance tempo)
- Rhythm accuracy: 90%+ correct note values and rest placements
- Key signature awareness: Members understand accidentals and scale degrees
- Form awareness: Members can identify major sections (Opener, Ballad, Closer)

**Functional Benchmark:** All members can play/perform their parts with minimal errors at rehearsal tempo by end of set work

---

#### SET 2: Musical Phrasing and Expression
**Primary Set ID:** 46e433da-ad34-4135-8b29-67023111e4f7

**Purpose:** Develop musicality, phrasing shape, breath points, and interpretive nuance

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Phrase shaping shows clear arc (crescendo/decrescendo) matching musical line
- Breath points coordinated within sections (staggered breathing where appropriate)
- Stylistic articulation matches genre (march vs. ballad vs. contemporary)
- Members can sing/vocalize melodic phrases with appropriate expression
- Dynamic contrast reflects score markings with musical intent

**Functional Benchmark:** Musical performance shows mature interpretation beyond "notes and rhythms"

---

#### SET 3: Dynamics and Articulation
**Primary Set ID:** 40a9c1a8-622f-4713-b3a1-e550d4353c2a

**Purpose:** Control dynamic range, articulation clarity, and contrast in show repertoire

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Dynamic range spans pp to ff with 6+ levels of distinction
- Crescendo/decrescendo show gradual, controlled change (not sudden jumps)
- Articulation styles (legato, staccato, marcato, tenuto) are distinct and intentional
- Ensemble dynamic balance maintained (melody projects over harmony)
- Percussion articulation complements brass/wind phrasing

**Functional Benchmark:** Show music demonstrates professional dynamic control and articulation precision

---

#### SET 4: Section Integration and Handoffs
**Primary Set ID:** 6d1dd6d2-15ee-4b46-8955-2c7e20acbc48

**Purpose:** Coordinate musical handoffs, counterpoint, and inter-section coordination

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Melodic handoffs are seamless (no gaps or overlaps)
- Counterpoint lines maintain independence while staying locked rhythmically
- Accompaniment figures support melody without overpowering
- Front ensemble and battery integration shows unified rhythmic feel
- Guard work reflects musical phrasing and energy

**Functional Benchmark:** Music flows as unified composition, not isolated section work

## MOVEMENT 3: VISUAL PROGRAM CONSTRUCTION
**Movement ID:** 9df236e1-e98b-43e7-a1a2-0b63ee309688

### Canonical Sets (Consolidated View):

#### SET 1: Drill Design and Charting
**Primary Set ID:** d756f722-94ec-4d9d-b73a-9e99ca0ee860

**Purpose:** Create and teach drill formations, count structures, coordinate sheet reading

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- 100% of performers understand coordinate sheet system and can read dot assignments
- Grid system understood: 4-step (high school) or 8-to-5 (collegiate) intervals accurate within 6 inches
- Count structure mastered: Members can mark time through transitions and hit sets on time
- Formation integrity: Set arrivals show <12 inch variance from assigned coordinates
- Transition paths are efficient and avoid collisions

**Functional Benchmark:** Ensemble can learn and execute new drill sets with minimal walk-through repetitions

---

#### SET 2: Movement Vocabulary Development
**Primary Set ID:** c133da01-3cbd-468c-a20a-76dc9cd87ce4

**Purpose:** Establish body movement language - posture, carriage, visual style

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Unified carriage style across ensemble (horn angles, body posture, head position)
- Movement vocabulary catalog established (jazz runs, body angles, turns)
- Execution shows visual clarity and commitment (no tentative movement)
- Style consistency maintained during full-tempo drill
- Visual timing matches musical phrasing

**Functional Benchmark:** Ensemble shows cohesive visual identity with professional body control

---

#### SET 3: Guard Choreography and Equipment Work
**Primary Set ID:** 78364f47-8d9e-448d-9e91-f6daa792d6d5

**Purpose:** Develop guard-specific choreography integrated with show concept

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- Choreography reflects show theme and musical energy
- Guard formations coordinate with full ensemble drill
- Equipment changes executed safely and efficiently
- Visual storytelling clear and intentional

**Functional Benchmark:** Guard program enhances show concept and integrates seamlessly with full ensemble

---

#### SET 4: Spacing and Form Accuracy
**Primary Set ID:** 6feaf986-7065-4f79-b342-cdbbeb369b0c

**Purpose:** Achieve precise form alignment, interval spacing, and visual clarity

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Intervals (file-to-file spacing) consistent within 6 inches across forms
- Forms show geometric integrity (straight lines, even curves, symmetry)
- Depth perception awareness: performers adjust to maintain visual plane
- "Picture frames" clean: members aware of their role in creating visual shapes
- Real-time adjustment skills: performers can correct spacing without staff intervention

**Functional Benchmark:** Forms create visually stunning impact from press box perspective
